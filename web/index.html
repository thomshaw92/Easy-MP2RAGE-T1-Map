About this tool & how it works

What it does. Computes a quantitative T1 map (in ms) from a Siemens MP2RAGE acquisition, corrected for transmit-field (B1⁺) inhomogeneity using either a SA2RAGE scan or a separately acquired B1 map (e.g. a Siemens tfl B1 map). It is a Rust→WebAssembly port of the T1-mapping parts of J. P. Marques' scripts, validated to match the reference pipeline to machine precision.

Outputs. B1-corrected T1 map (ms), the uncorrected T1 map, a relative B1 map (computed from your SA2RAGE — a standalone output you can keep), the B1-corrected UNI, and a parameters.json. The result panel overlays a pre/post (uncorrected vs corrected) T1 histogram, and everything downloads as NIfTI.

Privacy. Everything runs inside this browser tab via WebAssembly. Your images are never uploaded to any server — there is no server. You can disconnect from the network and it still works.

How to use. (1) Drop your NIfTI files and tag their roles. (2) Confirm the sequence parameters (7T/3T presets provided; dcm2niix .json sidecars auto-fill them). (3) Compute, preview, and download the T1/B1 maps as NIfTI. Inputs needed: the UNI image, INV2 (for the brain mask), and a B1 source — a 2-volume SA2RAGE or a B1 map.

Research software, provided as-is with no warranty. Sanity-check the maps and confirm the parameters against your protocol. Cite Marques 2010 (NeuroImage), Marques & Gruetter 2013 (PLoS ONE), Eggenschwiler 2012 (MRM).
